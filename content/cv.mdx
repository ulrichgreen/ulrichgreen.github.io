## Work

Ulrich Green is a product engineer and designer with eight years of experience building software that is meant to be used by people. The distinction sounds obvious. In practice it is not.

He spent four years at a mid-sized fintech company where he owned the design system from its inception as a shared Figma library through its maturity as a documented, versioned component library consumed by six product teams. The work was as much organisational as technical: writing contribution guidelines, running design crits, making the case to engineering leadership that consistency was worth the maintenance cost. It was.

Before that he worked at a small product studio where projects arrived as vague briefs and left as shipped software, usually within eight weeks. The pace was instructive. He learned to identify the single load-bearing decision in any design problem, make it quickly, and build around it rather than toward it.

He has written production code in TypeScript, Python, and Go. He has designed in Figma, prototyped in the browser, and conducted enough user research to have a healthy skepticism of user research. He holds the view that the best product decisions are made by people who can read a database query and a typographic hierarchy on the same afternoon.

## Selected Projects

_Ledger Design System_ — A component library and documentation site serving six product teams at Carrow Financial. Reduced design-to-development handoff time by roughly half. Built in React with a custom Storybook configuration and a contribution model that kept component count under control.

_Fieldwork_ — An internal tool for a logistics company that replaced a spreadsheet-based dispatch process. Designed and built end-to-end in six weeks. Still in daily use three years later.

_This site_ — A static site built with Make, MDX, React server rendering, and selective islands. See the [colophon](./colophon.html).
